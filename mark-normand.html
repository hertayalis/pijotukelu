    Mark Normand Early Career and Standup Shows

    Mark found his passion for comedy while completing his bachelor’s degree from the University of New Orleans and gradually started performing standup shows in his hometown in 2006. Slowly, he amassed popularity as a comedian because of his hilarious jokes and comedy acts.

    With increasing popularity as a comedian, he began to perform in various comedy festivals including Seattle International Comedy Competition, Boston Comedy Festival, Melbourne International Comedy Festival, and many others. In 2013, Mark received initial global fame as a New Face at Just for Laughs in Montreal, Canada.

    In the same year, he won the Carolines on Broadway’s March Madness competition after beating 63 other comedians. Subsquently, The Village Voice named him the Best Comedian of 2013. In 2019 a popular comedian named Jerry Seinfeld praised and appreciated Mark by saying “the best young up and coming comic that we should keep our eyes on”.

    As a comedian, Mark has appeared and performed in various international and national Tv and comedy shows including Last Comic Standing, MTV, The Late Late Show with James Corden, The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon, six times on Conan, TruTv, and many others.

    Mark Normand Album and Songs

    Apart from being a popular comedian, Mark is a popular musician, who released his debut album titled “Still Got It” in 2014 along with Comedy Central Records. In 2017, he released his hour-long comedy central special titled “Don’t Be Yourself”, which was loved globally. His latest album came in 2020 titled “Out to Lunch”, which also performed well globally.
