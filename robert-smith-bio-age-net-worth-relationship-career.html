    English singer, songwriter, and musician Robert James Smith, better known by his stage name Robert Smith, is most renowned for helping to form the rock group The Cure with his brother in 1976.

    He was the band’s lead vocalist, guitarist, multi-instrumentalist, lyricist, principal songwriter, and only member who was always there.

    He is well known for his distinctive voice, approach to playing the guitar, and stage persona—the latter two of which had a significant impact on the emergence of the goth subculture in the 1980s.In 2019, he was inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame as a member of the Cure.

    How did Robert Smith start his Professional Career?

    At the age of fourteen, he began his musical career with the high school group “The Crawley Goat Band,” which featured his sister Janet, brother Richard, and a few of his pals.

    Later, in 1976, he joined Michael Dempsey, Pearl Thompson, Laurence Tolhurst, and Smith to establish the band “Easy Cure,” with Smith serving as a singer and lyricist. After Pearl Thompson was fired from the band in 1978, the group changed its name to “The Cure.”

    The band recorded the singles “Killing an Arab” and “10:15 Saturday Night” between 1978 and 1979. They also made available “Three Imaginary Boys,” their debut record.

    In 1979, he and Steve Severin of Siouxsie and the Banshees embarked on a promotional tour of the United Kingdom in support of their second album, “Join Hands.” Following Michael Dempsey’s departure from the band in 1980, keyboardist Matthieu Hartly and bassist Simon Gallup joined.

    He was responsible for overseeing “The Cure’s” whole recording process from July 1982 until February 1985. In 1984, he also recorded the solo album “The Top,” on which Smith performed all the instrumentation alone but for the drums.

    He also collaborated with The Cure on new albums during the year 2000. Furthermore, “Bloodflowers” (2000), “The Cure” (2004), and “4:13 Dream” (2008) are among them.

    He covered “Very Good Advice” from the 1951 Alice in Wonderland movie for the 2010 album Almost Alice, which served as a companion release to Tim Burton’s version of the story. Additionally, “Pirate Ships” from 1989 was released on CD for the first time.

    He provided the vocals for Paul Hartnoll’s song “Please” from the album 8:58 in 2015. The Twilight Sad released a cover of “There’s a Girl in the Corner” by Smith on June 15, 2015.

    He also provided the vocals for the 2015 track “In All Worlds,” which is taken from Eat Static’s album Dead Planet.
