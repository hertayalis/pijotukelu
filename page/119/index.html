Everything About Chaneque: Mexican Folklore Legendary Creatures

25 Jun 2024, 00:00

Now, imagine a small creature, only about knee-high, with a playful twinkle in its eyes and an air of mischief. This, my friend, is a Chanekeh, Chaneque or Ohuican Chaneque (pronounced cha-neh-keh). They are one of the most intriguing characters in Mexican folklore. The word “Chaneque” comes from the Nahuatl language, spoken by the Aztecs, and it means ‘inhabitants of dangerous places.’ Many people believe that they live in the forests, near rivers, or any place where nature is wild and untamed.
